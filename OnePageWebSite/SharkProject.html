Fun Facts About Sharks
Until the 16th century they were known to seafarers are "sea dogs" Sharks can go into a trance. When you flip a shark upside down they go into a trance like state called tonic immobility
Shark bite fatalities only occur about six times per year. That's in comparison to the 100 million sharks that are killed by humans every year Each whale shark's spot pattern is unique as a fingerprint
Sharks generally avoid humans and only interact out of curiosity, in defense when we threaten them or invade their territory, or if they confuse humans for their normal prey Most of today's sharks developed 64 million years ago, same time as dinosaurs
Sharks are most active during dusk/dawn Baby sharks are called pups
As predators, sharks are critically important to maintaining healthy marine ecosystems. Predators keep populations of prey species strong by feeding on the weak and the sick, and they keep prey populations in check Many sharks are important prey for other wildlife from marine mammals, seabirds, and other fish (including other sharks)
